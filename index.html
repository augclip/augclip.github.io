Touch Only What Needs Touching: Novel Evaluation Protocol on Manipulation Abilities of Text-Guided Manipulation Models

Abstract

Recent advancements in multi-modal generative models have led to the development of text-guided image editing models, which have gained popularity for creative and artistic purposes. Well-manipulated samples must follow the text prompt while maintaining the original image's essence. However, existing metrics determine only one of these two aspects. In this paper, we propose a novel metric \texttt{AugCLIP} that simultaneously evaluates editing and preserving according to the relative importance of the visual factors created by a large language model. We also propose a new benchmark dataset, SameSwap, to evaluate the ability of the manipulation model to identify the area to be manipulated, constructed with four different situations. Finally, we provide an analysis of different kinds of manipulation models regarding visual factors.

Sameswap

We introduce SameSwap, which is a collection pairs of image and text prompt that describes a desired change of an image. We intentionally create confusing circumstances where the objects in an image are the same kinds(same noun) or share the same attributes(same attr). When creating target text prompts, we propose two distinct cases: swap noun and swap attr. In total, we conducted four scenarios: same attr swap attr, same attr swap noun, same noun swap attr, same noun swap noun.
